About Me

A student designer who cares about people first.

I enjoy turning everyday usability problems into clear, testable interface ideas. My HCI process starts with observing real user needs, then moves through sketches, wireframes, prototypes, feedback, and iteration.

Accessibility

I design with readable contrast, keyboard-friendly structure, meaningful labels, and inclusive content patterns.

Clarity

I organize information so users can quickly understand what matters, where they are, and what they can do next.

Research

I use interviews, task flows, and usability tests to replace assumptions with evidence from real behavior.

Iteration

I refine designs through feedback cycles, focusing on simpler interactions and better decision support.
